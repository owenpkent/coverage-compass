## Strategy in one paragraph

This is pitched as a **panel** (the format you chose) under the **Accessibility** theme, with Collaboration, Interoperability, and Transformation woven in. ### Your lived experience is the strongest card

For an Accessibility-themed session, the most powerful fact in this whole application is that you are not an outsider building for disabled people. You are a lifelong wheelchair user with spinal muscular atrophy, on Colorado Medicaid yourself, who directs your own attendant care through CDASS: the exact support this tool defends. FormFest explicitly says it wants to "listen to real users." You are one.

Both strengthening moves are now applied:

- **The description opens in your own voice.** It starts "I have used a wheelchair my whole life. I direct my own attendant care through Colorado Medicaid's CDASS program: the support that gets me out of bed." That is the strongest hook available, because the builder and the end user are the same person, and the stakes are concrete (the care that gets you out of bed), not abstract.
- **Your CDASS participation is named** in both the description and the bio, and it ties directly to your working CDASS Enroll proof of concept. The acronym is glossed in plain terms ("I direct my own attendant care") so a non-Colorado audience follows it.
- **Pre-population is the marquee forms value.** The description now leads with annual reapplication: 80-page forms re-asking the same facts every year, including the CDASS care-hours worksheet (the itemized minutes-per-task care plan). The tool pre-fills next year from what you already filed. For a conference about reducing form burden, this is the most resonant possible pitch, and it extends naturally to caseworkers.

FormFest's "submitter demographics" section is about showcasing diverse perspectives, and disability is central to that. There is no explicit disability field on the form, so your lived experience lands through the bio, the description, and (if relevant) the presenter-accommodation field. As a wheelchair user you may have genuine presenter access needs worth listing there; it is planning-only and does not affect selection.

### Working session description (198 words, under the 200 limit)

> I have used a wheelchair my whole life. I direct my own attendant care through Colorado Medicaid's CDASS program: the support that gets me out of bed. To keep it, I reapply every year, re-entering the same facts across forms that can run 80 pages, where a missed deadline can end coverage. Most disabled people already qualify; they lose coverage anyway through procedural disenrollment, a paperwork failure, not an eligibility failure. Arkansas proved the risk in 2018, when thousands lost coverage they qualified for. I am building Coverage Compass with the Colorado Cross-Disability Coalition (CCDC), a confirmed, disability-led partner. It organizes your evidence once and reuses it: drop in an HCPF or PEAK letter for a plain-language summary and the deadline, then pre-fill next year's renewal and care-hours worksheet from what you already filed. It is WCAG 2.2 AA, screen-reader-first, bilingual, and runs in your browser. The same engine can serve caseworkers, and other states. Why now: letters mail August 2026, first impact lands January 2027, and roughly 20 states follow. Joining me are a front-line CCDC advocate and an invited Colorado eligibility partner. Expect a live walkthrough, cross-talk, Q&A, and an advocate-editable rule-library template to take home.

*This version leads with the angle a government-forms audience cares about most: annual reapplication makes you re-enter the same facts across forms that can run dozens of pages, so the tool pre-fills next year's renewal and the CDASS care-hours worksheet from what you already filed. Notice triage (drop a letter, get a summary and deadline) stays in as a feature, and the caseworker and multi-state extensions broaden the appeal. Your specific care-plan details are deliberately kept out of this public text.*
