Five ways to help someone file an appeal

The appeal flow (v0.3) is structurally different from notice triage. Triage is one sitting; appeals can take days. Triage explains a letter; appeals build an artifact. And the output is always a draft for a CCDC advocate, never a direct filing with the state.

The scenario all five mockups use

Jane M. (HCBS waiver, SSDI) got a procedural termination letter dated May 15, 2026. Her coverage ends June 30, 2026. She has 10 days to request continuation of benefits and 60 days to file the appeal. Today is May 16, 2026.

The two-clock urgent banner is the same across all five so you can focus on the shape of the workflow.

Appeal mockups

A1

Progress tracker, linear stages

Six steps. Save and come back. One screen at a time.

Legible, screen-reader-easy. Can feel like a tax form on a bad day.

 A2

"Your case" dashboard

Cards for each part. Do them in any order. Progress visible at a glance.

Matches multi-sitting reality. Needs local persistence to be real.

 A3

Writing to a CCDC advocate

Short guided conversation. The draft fills in as you answer.

Honest about who is filing. Live regions need care for screen readers.

 A4

Letter and draft, side by side

Denial on the left, growing appeal draft on the right.

Great for advocate-and-member pairs. Hard on small screens.

 A5

Fill in the letter

The draft is the UI. Click a blank to answer the question for it.

Concrete and motivating. Inline editing is hard to do well accessibly.

What is the same across all five

  • The two-clock banner at the top: continuation-of-benefits clock first, appeal-deadline clock second.
  • Output is a draft sent to a CCDC advocate. The app never files anything with the state.
  • Saved on the user's device only. No account, no telemetry.
  • Plain English target. Legal phrasing is the advocate's job.
