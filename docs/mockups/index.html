CC Coverage Compass UI brainstorm, May 2026

UI brainstorms across the three Medicaid events

The tool covers three life events: reporting (monthly or periodic proof you still qualify), reapplication (annual renewals and new applications), and appeals (responding to a denial or termination). A fourth area, notice triage, is the shared entry point: drop a letter, find out which of the three flows applies.

Each section below holds a few takes on the same flow. Same content, same trust posture; the only thing that changes is the shape of the interaction. Open one and pretend you are a CCDC member who just opened the app for the first time.

All mockups share shared.css. The shared sheet aims at WCAG 2.2 AA with AAA contrast on body text, respects prefers-reduced-motion, prefers-color-scheme, and prefers-contrast, keeps tap targets at 48px or more, and uses semantic HTML.

Notice triage (entry point)

5 mockups

A letter shows up from HCPF or PEAK. The user does not always know if it is a renewal, a termination, or something else. Triage figures that out in plain language and points to the right flow.

T1

Single screen drop target

Whole viewport is a drop zone. Result renders in place.

Lowest cognitive load. Secondary inputs harder to surface.

 T2

Linear wizard

One question per step. Big buttons. Clear progress.

Excellent for screen readers. Feels slow for repeat use.

 T3

Conversational

Short interview, large choice buttons, not freeform chat.

Lowers fear. Live-region churn is risky for AT.

 T4

Letter and translation, side by side

Original on the left, plain meaning on the right.

Best for advocate-and-member pairs. Hard on small screens.

 T5

Print-first one-pager

Output looks like a printable document from the start.

Matches paper-based mental model. Large print for free.

Event 1: reporting

2 mockups

Ongoing proof that you still qualify. For the majority on an exemption, that means a periodic re-attestation (often monthly). For the small minority who must work, it means tracking the 80-hour target. Both versions are low-friction by design: the heavy lift is the once-a-year renewal, not this.

R1

Monthly check-in (exemption majority)

One question, two big buttons: "nothing changed" or "something changed". 2-second flow.

Lowest possible friction. Risk: people tap "nothing changed" reflexively without thinking.

 R2

Work hours log (work-hours minority)

Weekly bars, running total toward 80, proof pills (pay stubs, photos).

Tangible progress. Adds repeat-use complexity; needs persistence.

Event 2: reapplication

2 mockups

Renewals (usually once a year) and new applications. The heaviest practical lift for CCDC's constituency, and where procedural disenrollment usually happens. The two takes split the same task by what they put at the center: the documents, or the calendar.

N1

Packet builder

Documents organized by section, with "need this / have this" status and an overall progress bar.

Concrete and motivating. Big screen surface; needs IndexedDB to be real.

 N2

Deadline-anchored plan

The renewal date is the hero. Steps are scheduled backward from it with built-in buffer.

Calms time anxiety. Less useful if the user is already past key milestones.

Event 3: appeals

5 mockups

A denial, termination, or care reduction arrived. Triage is one sitting; appeals can take days. Triage explains; appeals build an artifact. The output is always a draft for a CCDC advocate, never a direct filing with the state. Two clocks run at once (continuation of benefits, then the appeal itself). The scenario across all five: Jane M. (HCBS waiver, SSDI), procedural termination dated May 15, 2026, coverage ends June 30. Today is May 16.

A1

Progress tracker, linear stages

Six steps. Save and come back. One screen at a time.

Legible and SR-easy. Can feel like a tax form on a bad day.

 A2

"Your case" dashboard

Cards for each part. Do them in any order. Progress visible.

Matches multi-sitting reality. Needs local persistence.

 A3

Writing to a CCDC advocate

Short guided conversation. The draft fills in as you answer.

Honest about who is filing. Live regions need care for AT.

 A4

Letter and draft, side by side

Denial on the left, growing draft on the right.

Great for advocate-and-member pairs. Hard on small screens.

 A5

Fill in the letter

The draft is the UI. Click a blank to answer the question for it.

Concrete and motivating. Inline editing is hard to do accessibly.

How to evaluate these

  • Open each mockup in a new tab. Try them with the keyboard only (Tab, Shift+Tab, Enter, Space).
  • Switch your OS to dark mode and reload. All should remain readable.
  • Turn on "reduce motion" in your OS. Nothing should animate.
  • Zoom to 200% in the browser. Layout should not break.
  • Run a screen reader (NVDA, VoiceOver, or TalkBack) for a minute on each.
